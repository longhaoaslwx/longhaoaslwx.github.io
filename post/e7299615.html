cart

单词解释

  1. n.(旧时的)马车，木车
    A cart is an old-fashioned wooden vehicle that is used for transporting goods or people. Some carts are pulled by animals.

  2. v.(常指费力地)运送,运输，拉
    If you cart things or people somewhere, you carry them or transport them there, often with difficulty.

  3. n.小型机动车
    A cart is a small vehicle with a motor.
  4. n.(超市等提供的)购物车,手推车
    A cart or a shopping cart is a large metal basket on wheels which is provided by shops such as supermarkets for customers to use while they are in the shop.
  5. phrase.前后颠倒;本末倒置
    If you say that someone is putting the cart before the horse, you mean that they are doing things in the wrong order.

计科专业释义：匣

例句

…a country where horse-drawn carts far outnumber cars.
马车的数量远远超过汽车的国家

I’ve been trying to cut down on the stuff that I cart around with me.
我一直想减少随身带的东西。

organize

单词解释

  1. v. If you organize an event or activity, you make sure that the necessary arrangements are made. 组织；安排；筹备

  2. v. If you organize something that someone wants or needs, you make sure that it is provided.提供，准备（想要或所需之物）

  3. v. If you organize a set of things, you arrange them in an ordered way or give them a structure.整理；使有条理；使具结构
  4. v. If you organize yourself, you plan your work and activities in an ordered, efficient way.规划（自己）；使有序高效
  5. v. If someone organizes workers or if workers organize, they form a group or society such as a trade union in order to have more power.（使）组织起来

计科专业释义：组织

例句

The initial mobilisation was well organised.

我来安排运输。

investment

单词解释

  1. n.投资
    Investment is the activity of investing money.

  2. n.投资金额;投资物
    An investment is an amount of money that you invest, or the thing that you invest it in.

  3. n.有用的投资物;值得买的东西
    If you describe something you buy as an investment, you mean that it will be useful, especially because it will help you to do a task more cheaply or efficiently.
  4. n. (时间、精力的)投入
    Investment of time or effort is the spending of time or effort on something in order to make it a success.

例句

…investment bankers.

credit

单词解释

  1. n. 赊购;赊欠
    If you are allowed credit, you are allowed to pay for goods or services several weeks or months after you have received them.

  2. n. 结余;存款
    If someone or their bank account is in credit, their bank account has money in it.

  3. v. (给账户)存(钱);把…记入贷方
    When a sum of money is credited to an account, the bank adds that sum of money to the total in the account.
  4. n. 存款金额;贷记
    A credit is a sum of money which is added to an account.
  5. n. (给某人的)一笔钱
    A credit is an amount of money that is given to someone.
  6. n. 赞扬;称赞;认可
    If you get the credit for something good, people praise you because you are responsible for it, or are thought to be responsible for it.
  7. v. 认为是…的功劳;把…归于
    If people credit someone with an achievement or if it is credited to them, people say or believe that they were responsible for it.
  8. v. 认为…有(某种品质)
    If you credit someone with a quality, you believe or say that they have it.
  9. n.为…赢得好感的人(或事物)
    If you say that someone is a credit to someone or something, you mean that their qualities or achievements will make people have a good opinion of the person or thing mentioned.
  10. v. 相信;信任
    If you cannot credit something, you cannot believe that it is true.
  11. n. 演职员表;摄制人员名单
    The list of people who helped to make a film, a CD, or a television programme is called the credits .
  12. n. (某些大学获得学位所必需的)学分，学习单元
    A credit is a successfully completed part of a higher education course. At some universities and colleges you need a certain number of credits to be awarded a degree.
  13. phrase.使值得赞扬(或仰慕)
    If you say that something does someone credit, you mean that they should be praised or admired because of it.
  14. phrase. （某人的成绩或优点）值得表扬，应予肯定
    If you say ‘ credit where credit‘s due’, you are admitting that you ought to praise someone for something that they have done or for a good quality that they possess.
  15. phrase. 相信…具有(某种优良品质);称赞;认可
    To give someone credit for a good quality means to believe that they have it.
  16. phrase. (常用于提出缺点之后)就优点而言
    You say on the credit side in order to introduce one or more good things about a situation or person, usually when you have already mentioned the bad things about them.
  17. phrase. 值得赞扬
    If something is to someone’s credit, they deserve praise for it.
  18. phrase. (成就)归于…；在…名下
    If you already have one or more achievements to your credit, you have achieved them.

例句

The group can’t get credit to buy farming machinery…
这些人无法赊购到农用机械。

You can ask a dealer for a discount whether you pay cash or buy on credit.
无论是付现还是赊购，都可以要求经销商打折。

detective

单词解释

  1. n. （警队或私人）侦探
    A detective is someone whose job is to discover what has happened in a crime or other situation and to find the people involved. Some detectives work in the police force and others work privately.

  2. n. （用于警察头衔）(英国英语中常用于 constable，sergeant 等词前）侦探，（美国英语中单独使用)警探
    In Britain ‘detective’ is used before words such as ‘constable’ or ‘sergeant’, and in the U.S. the word ‘detective’ is used on its own, to indicate that a police officer is a member of the department concerned with investigating crimes.

  3. adj.(小说或故事)侦探的，破案的
    A detective novel or story is one in which a detective tries to solve a crime.
  4. phrase. 调查;探究
    If you do some detective work, you do something to find out more about a subject or situation that puzzles you.

例句

She hired a private detective in an attempt to find her daughter.
她雇了一名私人侦探，试图找到自己的女儿。

Detectives are appealing for witnesses who may have seen anything suspicious…
探员呼吁看到任何可疑情况的人进行举报。

  1. n.（尤指艺术表达中的）低调处理，克制

    She trimmed back my flowery language, drew lines through my exclamation marks and argued for the value of restraint in expression.
    她删掉我使用的那些华丽辞藻，用线划去感叹号并且告诉我限制性表达的价值。

来源：2015年12月大学英语四级考试真题（三）

  1. n.控制，限制

    Until now, one of the biggest restraints on consumer spending has been a debt aftereffect.
    到目前为止，对消费者支出的最大限制之一就是债务余波。

词跟及同根词

同根词 释义
restrained adj.克制的，受限制的；拘谨的
restrainer n.[摄] 抑制剂（尤指摄影中用的溴化钾等）；限制器；控制者
restrained v.抑制；约束（restrain的过去分词）
restrain vt.抑制，控制；约束；制止

tolerant

单词解释

  1. adj. 宽容的;容忍的
    If you describe someone as tolerant, you approve of the fact that they allow other people to say and do as they like, even if they do not agree with or like it.

  2. adj. （植物、动物、机器）能耐…的，有耐受性的
    If a plant, animal, or machine is tolerant of particular conditions or types of treatment, it is able to bear them without being damaged or hurt.

例句

They need to be tolerant of different points of view…
他们需要容忍不同的观点。

Other changes include more tolerant attitudes to unmarried couples having children.
其他变化包括对非婚夫妇生育更为宽容的态度。

真题例句

  1. adj.（植物、动物或机器）能耐的，耐（受）性的

    The big difference between “drought-tolerant“ plants and these tough plants: metabolism.
    “耐旱”植物和这些生命力顽强的植物之间的一个很大的区别是：新陈代谢。

来源：2018年6月大学英语六级考试真题（二）

  1. adj.忍受的；容忍的，宽容的，宽恕的

    They are tolerant of political and religious nonsense.
    他们容忍政治和宗教的胡说八道。

来源：2014年6月六级真题

词跟及同根词

词根：toler=endure,表示“容忍”

同根词 释义
tolerate 释义：v. 容忍, 忍受, 容许, 包容
tolerant 释义：adj. 忍受的; 容忍的, 宽容的, 宽恕的; 能耐的, 耐(受)性的
tolerable toler+able，释义：adj. 尚好的, 过得去的, 还可以的, 可接受的
intolerant 　in不+tolerant心胸宽大的，释义：adj. 不容忍的, 褊狭的, 不容异说的, 偏执的 n. 不容忍的人, 偏狭的人

笔记扩展

infect

单词解释

  1. v. 传染;侵染;感染
    To infect people, animals, or plants means to cause them to have a disease or illness.

  2. v. 污染
    To infect a substance or area means to cause it to contain harmful germs or bacteria.

  3. v. 使受影响;感染
    When people, places, or things are infected by a feeling or influence, it spreads to them.
  4. v. 使(计算机)感染病毒
    If a virus infects a computer, it affects the computer by damaging or destroying programs.

计科专业释义:(计算机病毒)使…受传染

例句

…people infected with HIV.
感染艾滋病病毒的人们

…objects used by an infected person.
被感染者使用过的物品

真题例句

  1. v.污染（空气、水等）

    When the rats detect the smell, they stop and rub their legs to indicate a sample is infected.
    当老鼠检测到气味时，它们会停下来并揉搓双腿以表明样本被污染。

来源：2017年12月四级真题

  1. v.【喻】（负面感受或思想）感染，影响，传播给（某人）

    The college admission essay contains the grandest American themes ― status anxiety, parental piety, intellectual standards — and so it is only a matter of time before it becomes infected by the country’s culture of excessive concern with self-esteem.
    大学入学论文包括美国社会最重大的主题——身份焦虑，孝顺父母，智力标准——所以这个国家的文化中过分关注的自尊问题迟早会影响这些主题。

来源：2013年12月大学英语六级考试真题（一）

词跟及同根词

词根:fac, fect, fic, fig, fea=make,do,表示“做，制作”
词根助记：infect:in进入+fect→做进去→传染进去

同根词 释义
infect in进入+fect→做进去→传染进去,释义：v. 传染; 感染; 污染; 使受影响, 传播给某人; [计算机]感染病毒
effect ef出+fect→做出来→生效；效果,释义：n. 影响, 作用, 效果, 效应 v. 引起, 〈正式〉实现, 达成, 使…发生
effective effect +ive,释义：adj. 有效的；生效的
efficient ef出+fic+ent→能做出事来→有效率的,释义：adj. 效率高的，高效的
benefactor benefaction衍生词,释义：n. 施主, 捐款人, 赞助人
facile fac+ile能…的,释义：adj. 轻率作出的, 不动脑筋的, 轻易可得的, 得来容易的
forfeit 释义：v. （因犯错）丧失 n. 罚金, 没收物 adj. 被罚的, 被没收的
magnificence mag大+fic=伟大的作品=壮丽，宏伟,释义：n. 壮丽

considerable

    Considerable means great in amount or degree.

Vets’ fees can be considerable, even for routine visits.

Doing it properly makes considerable demands on our time…
把这事做好要耗费我们相当多的时间。

  1. adj.相当大的；相当多的；相当重要的

    They show considerable differences in their decision-making abilities.

consider vi.考虑；认为；细想
consider vt.考虑；认为；考虑到；细想
