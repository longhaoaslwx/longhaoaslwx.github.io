theme

单词解释

  1. n. (作品、谈话、讨论的)主题，题目
    A theme in a piece of writing, a talk, or a discussion is an important idea or subject that runs through it.

  2. n. (艺术品、文学作品的)主题，中心思想
    A theme in an artist’s work or in a work of literature is an idea in it that the artist or writer develops or repeats.

  3. n.(音乐的)主旋律
    A theme is a short simple tune on which a piece of music is based.
  4. n. (电影、电视节目、广播节目的)主题音乐，主题曲
    Theme music or a theme song is a piece of music that is played at the beginning and end of a film or of a television or radio programme.

例句

‘That’s another element in choosing a style of writing, you see,’ he says, returning to his main theme…
“瞧，那是选择写作风格时要考虑的又一个因素，”他又回到了主题上。

The need to strengthen the family has been a recurrent theme for the Prime Minister.
巩固家庭关系是首相屡次谈到的话题。

真题例句

  1. n.（文艺作品的）主题（思想）

    This bossy father figure became a common theme that recurred throughout Plath’s writing.
    这一专横的父亲形象是普拉斯文字中的常见主题，经常反复出现。

来源：2015年12月六级真题

  1. n.题目，主题

    The college admission essay contains the grandest American themes ― status anxiety, parental piety, intellectual standards — and so it is only a matter of time before it becomes infected by the country’s culture of excessive concern with self-esteem.
    大学入学论文包括美国社会最重大的主题——身份焦虑，孝顺父母，智力标准——所以这个国家的文化中过分关注的自尊问题迟早会影响这些主题。

来源：2013年12月大学英语六级考试真题（一）

单词解释

  1. n. (组织机构的)负责人，头儿，首领

  3. adj. (用于职位头衔)主管的，首席的

  4. adj. 主要的;最重要的

barely

单词解释

  1. adv. 刚刚；勉强；仅仅；几乎不
    You use barely to say that something is only just true or only just the case.

  2. adv. 刚刚…（就）
    If you say that one thing had barely happened when something else happened, you mean that the first event was followed immediately by the second.

例句

She was an elfin-like girl who looked barely 10 years old.
她是个鬼马小精灵，看起来也就刚满10岁。

His voice was barely audible…
他的声音几乎听不见。

来源：2018年6月大学英语四级考试真题（二）

margin

单词解释

  1. n. (尤指选票或比分的)差额，差数
    A margin is the difference between two amounts, especially the difference in the number of votes or points between the winner and the loser in an election or other contest.

  2. n. 页边空白;页边;白边
    The margin of a written or printed page is the empty space at the side of the page.

  3. n. 余地;富余
    If there is a margin for something in a situation, there is some freedom to choose what to do or decide how to do it.
  4. n. 边缘;边沿
    The margin of a place or area is the extreme edge of it.
  5. n. （社会、群体或活动中的）边缘地位，非主体地位
    To be on the margins of a society, group, or activity means to be among the least typical or least important parts of it.

例句

The margin in favor was 280-to-153.
赞成票以280票比153票的优势领先。

The Sunday Times remains the brand leader by a huge margin…
《星期日泰晤士报》仍以巨大的优势稳居报业龙头的地位。

attribute

单词解释

  1. v. 把…归因于;把…归咎于
    If you attribute something to an event or situation, you think that it was caused by that event or situation.

  2. v. 将（品质或特征）加于…;认为…为…所有
    If you attribute a particular quality or feature to someone or something, you think that they have got it.

  3. v.认为(文章、艺术品或评论）出自…
    If a piece of writing, a work of art, or a remark is attributed to someone, people say that they wrote it, created it, or said it.
  4. n. （人或物的）品质，特征
    An attribute is a quality or feature that someone or something has.

例句

Women tend to attribute their success to external causes such as luck.
女性倾向于将她们的成功归因于外部因素，比如运气。

People were beginning to attribute superhuman qualities to him.
人们开始觉得他超人一等。

真题例句

    “我们公司销售额近80%的同比下滑可以归因于用来应对寒冷天气的商品的短缺。”梅西百货的首席执行官泰瑞·伦德格伦在一次新闻发布会上说道。

来源：2017年6月大学英语六级考试真题（一）

    根据《大西洋月刊》上描述的一项2012年的研究，在评估待招聘的毕业生的所有因素中，大学声望的相对重要性排在最后，不如实习经历、在校期间的就业经历、大学所学专业、义工经历和参与的课外活动等因素排名靠前。

来源：2018年6月大学英语六级考试真题（三）

grant

单词解释

  1. n. 拨款;补助金
    A grant is an amount of money that a government or other institution gives to an individual or to an organization for a particular purpose such as education or home improvements.

  2. v. 授予；给予
    If someone in authority grants you something, or if something is granted to you, you are allowed to have it.

  3. v.承认
    If you grant that something is true, you accept that it is true, even though your opinion about it does not change.
  4. phrase.（因视作理所当然而）不把…当回事，对…不予重视（或不知感激）
    If you say that someone takes you for granted, you are complaining that they benefit from your help, efforts, or presence without showing that they are grateful.
  5. phrase. 认为…是理所当然的
    If you take something for granted, you believe that it is true or accept it as normal without thinking about it.
  6. phrase. 理所当然地认为
    If you take it for granted that something is the case, you believe that it is true or you accept it as normal without thinking about it.

计科专业释义:

  1. 授权 （引用：31 来源：数字版权管理系统机理与关键技术研究 ）
    Under most circumstances,access control procedure may be composed of two part processing functional component: authentication to all kinds of the parties concerned and grant the proper authorization to the parties involed for accessing the DRM protected multimedia resources.
    将数字版权管理系统中访问控制处理环节分解为两部分,即对各参与实体的身份验证及其使用操作请求授权。
  2. 授与
  3. 允许

例句

They’d got a special grant to encourage research…
他们得到一笔用以鼓励科研的特别经费。

Unfortunately, my application for a grant was rejected.
很遗憾，我的助学金申请被拒绝了。

  2. v. 有，拥有（值得骄傲的成就或财产）
    If someone or something can boast a particular achievement or possession, they have achieved or possess that thing.

例句

He’s boasted of being involved in the arms theft…
他吹嘘自己参与过盗窃军火的行动。

We remember our mother’s stern instructions not to boast.
我们谨记母亲不许我们在人前炫耀的严厉教诲。

真题例句

  1. v.（人，地方，事物）拥有（可引以自豪的特点）

    Italy boasts more UNESCO World Heritage Sites than any other country in the world, granting it considerable power and influence within the organization.
    意大利吹嘘自己享有的联合国教科文组织世界遗产比世界上其他任何一个国家都多，这授予了它在联合国教科文组织里享有相当大的权力和势力。

来源：2018年6月大学英语四级考试真题（二）

  1. v.自夸，自吹自擂，夸耀

    These companies, which long reaped big profits as textbook publishers, have boasted that their new online offerings, when pushed to students through universities they partner with, represent the future of the industry.
    这些长期以来以教科书出版商的身份获得巨额利润的公司吹嘘说，他们新的在线产品代表了该行业的未来，他们通过自己合作的大学，促使学生使用他们的在线产品。

来源：2018年6月大学英语四级考试真题（一）

词跟及同根词

词根：boast

同根词 释义
boastful adj.自夸的；自负的；喜夸耀的
boastfully adv.自夸地
boasting n.吹嘘
boaster n.自夸的人；大言不惭的人；吹牛者
boasting v.吹嘘，夸耀（boast的ing形式）

笔记扩展

1.boast of 吹牛，自夸
2.boast about 自夸，吹嘘

perform

单词解释

  1. v. 做，执行，履行（尤指复杂的任务或行动）
    When you perform a task or action, especially a complicated one, you do it.

  2. v.起…作用；有…功能
    If something performs a particular function, it has that function.

  3. v. 演出；表演；演奏
    If you perform a play, a piece of music, or a dance, you do it in front of an audience.
  4. v. （人）表现良好/很差；（事物）运转良好/糟糕
    If someone or something performs well, they work well or achieve a good result. If they perform badly, they work badly or achieve a poor result.

例句

We’re looking for people of all ages who have performed outstanding acts of bravery, kindness or courage…
我们正在寻找那些见义勇为、乐于助人、勇气可嘉的人，年龄不限。

Several grafts may be performed at one operation.
在一次手术中可能会进行多次植皮。

真题例句

  1. v.工作；运行；表现

    “An ultimate face recognition algorithm should perform with billions of people in a dataset,” the researchers wrote.
    研究人员写道：“最终的人脸识别算法应该在数十亿人的数据集中进行。”

来源：2018年6月大学英语六级考试真题（二）

  1. v.执行（任务）；完成；实施（行动）；履行（职责）

    Certainly, there’re benefits to a college major beyond the job a student can perform.
    当然，大学专业的益处可以超越一个学生可能从事的工作。

来源：2018年6月大学英语六级考试真题（二）

  1. v.演出，表演

    When performing, he positions his instruments in a way that is deliberately inconvenient, so that switching from guitar to organ mid-song involves a mad dash across the stage.
    演奏时，他故意把乐器放在不方便的位置，这样在弹吉他和用风琴演奏之间转换时，就需要在舞台上疯狂地跑来跑去。

来源：2013年12月大学英语六级考试真题（二）

词跟及同根词

词根:form=shape,表示“形状”
词根助记：perform:per完全，彻底+form形状→从头到尾做出形状→表演

同根词 释义
form 释义：n. 形式, 表格, 形状, 样子 v. 组成, 建立, 组织, 产生 linkv. 成为, 是
formal 释义：adj. 适合正式场合的，庄重的；正式的；合乎规矩的
perform per完全，彻底+form形状→从头到尾做出形状→表演,释义：v. 做, 进行; 演出, 表演; 执行, 履行; 工作; 运行; 表现
reform re再次+form→再形成→改造,释义：v. 改革，变革 n. 改革，变革
formality 释义：n. 例行公事, 正式手续, 遵守礼节
multiform multi多+form→多形式的,释义：adj. 多样的
malformation mal错误的+form,释义：n. （身体的）畸形
reformatory re再次+form→再形成→改,释义：n. 少年犯管教所, 青少年教养院 adj. 起改革[革新]作用的, 起改良作用的

笔记扩展

  1. perform an operation 动手术；做手术
  2. perform on 表演；上演；演奏
  3. opportunity to perform 实践的机会；表现机会
  4. perform maintenance 修理
  5. perform a contract 执行合同
